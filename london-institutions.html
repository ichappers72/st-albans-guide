The Six — Sign up to their mailing lists

01
Intelligence Squared →

The world's premier forum for live debate. Big thinkers argue both sides of the most pressing questions in politics, ethics, science and culture. Compelling even when you disagree with the result.

Debates Philosophy Geopolitics

intelligencesquared.com
02
LSE Public Lectures →

The London School of Economics opens its doors to the public. Heads of state, Nobel laureates, and leading thinkers on international relations, economics, and global affairs. Regularly free to attend.

Often Free Geopolitics

lse.ac.uk/events
03
The British Museum →

Beyond the exhibits lies a programme of talks, symposia, and events spanning the full breadth of human history. Free entry to the permanent collection, with ticketed events for special programmes.

Free Entry History

britishmuseum.org
04
Conway Hall — Ethical Society →

A historic Holborn venue dedicated to free thought, humanist ethics, and open inquiry since 1887. Talks covering philosophy, secularism, and the big moral questions. Genuinely welcoming to young people.

Philosophy Often Free

conwayhall.org.uk
05
Chatham House →

The Royal Institute of International Affairs — the gold standard for geopolitical analysis. Many events are open to the public, bringing together diplomats, analysts and policymakers on the world's most pressing issues.

Geopolitics International Affairs

chathamhouse.org
06
Gresham College →

Free public lectures since 1597 — yes, really. Professors cover history, science, law, music, and philosophy in accessible, hour-long talks. All lectures are also available free online. An extraordinary and underrated resource.

Always Free History Philosophy

gresham.ac.uk

The move

Sign up to the mailing list for each institution you like. Most send a weekly or monthly programme so you can pick and choose what catches your eye — no commitment, no cost, just show up when something looks good.
