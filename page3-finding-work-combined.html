Your CV — keep it simple

📝
One Page, No Exceptions

At 18 with limited work history, one tight page is exactly right. Clean and readable beats a padded two-pager every time.

🧑
Lead With a Personal Statement

Two or three sentences at the top about who you are. Tailor it to each employer — mention that you're a reader for the bookshops, or that you're reliable and local for supermarkets.

📋
Everything Counts as Experience

School responsibilities, any volunteering, helping out informally — all of it goes on. At entry level, employers want to see reliability and attitude, not a long work history.

Walking in — how to do it right

🕐
Timing Matters

Mid-week, mid-morning is ideal — roughly 10am–12pm, Tuesday to Thursday. Avoid Monday mornings, weekend lunchtimes, and late afternoons. You want someone with headspace to talk to you.

🤝
Ask for the Manager

Don't just hand your CV to whoever's at the till. Ask to speak to the duty manager. It shows initiative and means your CV lands in the right hands rather than a drawer under the counter.

💬
Have a Genuine Reason

For Books on the Hill: you're a reader, you love the shop. For Morrisons: you're local, you use the store. A real reason lands far better than "I'm looking for any job."

🔄
Follow Up After a Week

Pop back in after about a week if you haven't heard anything. A quick, friendly check-in shows initiative without being pushy. Most people don't bother — it sets you apart.

The order of attack

Books on the Hill first — walk in, be yourself, buy a book. Waterstones second. Oxfam for volunteering regardless. Morrisons and Sainsbury's online applications running in parallel. Don't wait for one to say no before trying the next.
