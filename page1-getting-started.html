First things first

Getting Started

The admin that actually matters. Get these done in the right order and everything else falls into place.

Home

Do these first — roughly in this order

📱
Day One
Get a UK SIM Card

Grab one at the airport or any high street phone shop. Start with pay-as-you-go — Giffgaff, SMARTY, or iD Mobile are all decent and cheap. You can always upgrade to a monthly plan once you're settled.

🏥
First Week
Register with a GP

Find your nearest NHS GP surgery and register online or in person using your grandparents' address. Free and straightforward. Sort this before you need it, not when you're ill. Once registered, download the NHS app for appointments and prescriptions.

nhs.uk — How to register with a GP →
📋
First Week
Register on the Electoral Roll

Takes five minutes on gov.uk. Use your grandparents' address. Good habit to get into and helps build a paper trail at your address, which you'll need for other admin.

gov.uk/register-to-vote →
📱
First Month
Set Up the NHS App

Once you're registered with a GP, download the NHS app. Book appointments, order repeat prescriptions, and access your health records — all from your phone.

nhs.uk/nhs-app →
📮
First Month
Build Up Proof of Address

Many things — opening bank accounts, getting a phone contract, applying for jobs — require two or more letters at your address. Get a letter from your GP, a bank statement, anything official. Start building this paper trail early.

🚗
When Ready
Apply for a Provisional Driving Licence

This is your starting point before you can take lessons or a theory test. Apply online via gov.uk — you'll need your passport (or birth certificate and National Insurance number if no passport), and a recent passport-style photo. The fee is £34, paid by card online. Your licence arrives by post within about a week.

gov.uk — Apply for a provisional licence →
📖
After Provisional Arrives
Theory Test — Book and Pass This First

You must pass your theory test before you can book a practical driving test. It has two parts: a multiple choice section (50 questions, pass mark 43) and a hazard perception video test. Book via gov.uk — costs £23. The DVSA app has official revision materials. Most people pass with 4–6 weeks of regular practice.

gov.uk — Book your theory test →
🚙
After Theory Test
Driving Lessons & Practical Test

Once you have a provisional licence you can start lessons. Look for a DVSA-approved instructor — check the official register on gov.uk. The practical test costs £62 (weekdays) or £75 (evenings and weekends). You can book it before you feel fully ready, but most instructors will advise you on timing. Average learner takes 45–47 hours of lessons.

gov.uk — Find an approved instructor → gov.uk — Book your practical test →
💻
When Working
HMRC Personal Tax Account

Once you start work, set up your HMRC online account. You can check your tax code, make sure you're not being overtaxed (common for young first-time workers), and manage your National Insurance record.

gov.uk/personal-tax-account →

Good to know

Unlike many countries, the UK has no system for registering your address with local authorities. No need to report to a council or police station — the GP, electoral roll, and bank account are all you need to establish yourself.
