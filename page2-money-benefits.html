Benefits — what you might be entitled to

💰
Universal Credit

The main working-age benefit. You can claim this if you're on a low income or between jobs — and even if you're working, you may still be entitled to a top-up if your earnings are low enough. Living with family rent-free will be factored in, so check the calculator before assuming you don't qualify.

Tax & payslips — things not to ignore

📄
Your Tax Code

When you start work your employer gives you a tax code which determines how much tax is deducted. Young first-time workers often end up on an emergency tax code and get overtaxed. Check your payslip — if your code is anything other than 1257L, query it with HMRC.

gov.uk/tax-codes →
📁
P45 and P60 — Don't Bin Them

A P45 is what you get when you leave a job. A P60 is a summary of your pay and tax for the year, issued every April. Both matter — keep them somewhere safe. You'll need them if you change jobs, claim a tax refund, or apply for certain things.

💻
HMRC Personal Tax Account

Your online dashboard for everything tax-related. Check your tax code, claim refunds, view your National Insurance record, and manage your details. Set it up as soon as you start work.

gov.uk/personal-tax-account →

Quick win

If you've been overtaxed at any point during the tax year — which runs April to April — you can claim a refund directly through your HMRC account. It happens more often than people realise, especially in a first job.
