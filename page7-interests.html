History — you're in the right city

🏛️
The Arc & Arc — St Albans History Society

The St Albans & Hertfordshire Architectural & Archaeological Society, founded 1845, with over 700 members. One of the oldest and largest local history societies in the country. Given that St Albans is built on the Roman city of Verulamium, the subject matter is genuinely extraordinary. Lectures, walks, and field trips throughout the year.

stalbanshistory.org →
🏺
Verulamium Museum

One of the finest Roman museums in Britain, right in Verulamium Park. Worth visiting as a visitor first — then look into whether they take volunteers. Working in a museum environment is excellent experience and the subject matter is hard to beat.

stalbansmuseums.org.uk →

Philosophy & big ideas

🤔
School of Philosophy St Albans

Running philosophy classes for adults in Hertfordshire for over 50 years. Covers the big questions — Stoicism, ethics, consciousness, meaning — in an accessible, discussion-based format. They also run a Philosophy Café online for broader debate. Perfect if you want to think out loud with other people.

schoolofphilosophystalbans.org →

Reading

📚
St Albans Central Library

Located on The Maltings in the city centre. Free membership, massive selection, and often runs reading groups and author events. Check the noticeboard and their online events calendar — it's more active than you'd expect.

Hertfordshire Libraries →
🎤
Waterstones Author Events

The St Albans branch regularly hosts author talks and signings. Worth signing up to their mailing list for upcoming events — a good way to hear writers you already love, or discover new ones.

waterstones.com/events →
