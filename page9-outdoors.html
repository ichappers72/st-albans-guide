On your doorstep — St Albans & local

🌳
St Albans area Free
Heartwood Forest

England's largest new continuous native forest, just north of St Albans near Sandridge. Over 600,000 trees, ancient woodland pockets, wildflower meadows, bluebells in spring, and 20km of paths and bridleways. A 5-mile loop takes about 2 hours. Free parking, free entry. The 305 and 357 buses pass through Sandridge — a short walk from the forest entrance.

St Albans Free
Verulamium Park & River Ver

100 acres of parkland right in the city, built on the Roman town of Verulamium. Walk along the River Ver — one of England's rarest chalk streams — past Roman walls, the ornamental lake, and wildlife. You can spot kingfishers and muntjac deer. A perfect thinking walk that starts five minutes from the city centre. Finish at Ye Olde Fighting Cocks, reputedly one of England's oldest pubs.

St Albans to Hatfield Free
The Alban Way

6.5 miles along a disused railway line from St Albans to Hatfield — flat, traffic-free, and full of hidden history. Old station platforms are visible in the undergrowth, and it passes through green corridors of wildlife. Good for walking or cycling. You can join it at Fleetville or from central St Albans.

St Albans circular Free
Three Burys Walk — Ver Valley

A 7.5-mile circular walk starting from the Verulamium Museum, heading north past Childwickbury and Redbournbury Mill on the River Ver. One of the best local routes — varied landscape, quiet countryside, and genuine sense of getting away from the city without going far.

A bit further — bus or train

Wheathampstead — 321 bus
Shaw's Corner Walk

A circular walk from Wheathampstead through Hertfordshire countryside to Shaw's Corner — the home of playwright George Bernard Shaw, now a National Trust property with a beautiful three-acre garden. About 5 miles, lovely rural paths. The house itself is fascinating for anyone interested in literature or history. Wheathampstead is on the 321 bus from The Ancient Briton.

nationaltrust.org.uk — Shaw's Corner →

Dunstable — 321 to Luton, then bus
Dunstable Downs & Whipsnade Tree Cathedral

Sweeping views across the Vale of Aylesbury from the highest point in Bedfordshire, with wildflowers in spring and summer. Nearby is the Whipsnade Tree Cathedral — an extraordinary outdoor space where trees have been planted in the shape of a cathedral, created as a memorial after the First World War. Quiet, unusual, and genuinely moving.

nationaltrust.org.uk — Dunstable Downs →
🏔️
Tring — train or 321 to Luton area
Tring Park & The Ridgeway

One of the largest woodland and parkland areas in Hertfordshire, set on the edge of the Chilterns. Wide open meadows, ancient beech woodland, and the Ridgeway — one of Britain's oldest roads, used for 5,000 years. The village of Aldbury nearby has a historic stocks and whipping post on the green. Well worth a half-day.

London outdoors — 35 minutes away

🏊
London Free
Hampstead Heath

320 acres of wild, hilly parkland in North London — genuinely feels nothing like the city. Free open-air swimming ponds (separate men's and mixed ponds), great views over London from Parliament Hill, and proper countryside walking. One of London's greatest secrets. Tube to Hampstead or Gospel Oak.

🦌
London Free
Richmond Park

2,500 acres of ancient parkland in Southwest London with hundreds of wild deer roaming freely. On a clear day the views across to St Paul's are extraordinary. Bring a book, find a spot, and forget you're in one of the world's busiest cities. Train from St Pancras to Waterloo, then train to Richmond.

🌲
London / Essex border Free
Epping Forest

Ancient forest on the edge of London with oak trees up to 800 years old. Take the Central line to the end and you're in genuine ancient woodland. The Oak Trail is a 6.6-mile circular route through some of the oldest trees — walking under those canopies is a rare, humbling experience.

Useful apps

📱
AllTrails & Komoot

Both are free GPS walking apps with thousands of rated, mapped local routes. AllTrails is more user-friendly for beginners; Komoot is slightly more serious and better for longer routes. Download both — between them they cover every walk mentioned on this page and thousands more. No need to plan in advance; just open the app and find something nearby.

alltrails.com → komoot.com →

Worth knowing

The Hertfordshire Way is a 170-mile circular long-distance path that passes right through St Albans. You can walk it in sections as day routes — each one takes you through a different slice of the county. A good long-term project for someone who likes having something to work towards.
