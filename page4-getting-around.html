Your six bus routes

Arriva Beds & Bucks — daily, early morning to midnight
Luton → Harpenden → St Albans → Watford

Your most versatile route. Heading north: Harpenden in ~15 min, Luton in ~40 min. Heading south: St Albans city centre and on to Watford. The backbone of your network — use it constantly.

X10
Uno — Limited Stop Express
Hatfield → St Albans → Luton (express, fewer stops)

A faster limited-stop express. Good when you want a quicker run to Luton or into the city centre. Also useful heading east to Hatfield — Galleria shopping centre and Hatfield Business Park.

721
Arriva Beds & Bucks
Luton → Harpenden → St Albans → Hemel Hempstead

Your direct route to Hemel Hempstead with no changes. Also covers Harpenden and Luton going the other way. Handy for Hemel's cinema, Riverside shopping, and leisure options.

305
Centrebus — Mon–Sat
St Albans City Centre → Colney Heath → Sandridge

Goes into St Albans city centre and out to Colney Heath and surrounding villages. Useful as a backup for getting into town. Runs Monday to Saturday — check timetable as frequency is limited.

Apps, tickets & money savers

📱
Download: Arriva, Uno & Intalink

The 321 and 721 are Arriva. The X10 is Uno. For everything else — including planning journeys across multiple operators — use the Intalink app, which covers all Hertfordshire buses in one place.

arrivabus.co.uk → unobus.info → intalink.org.uk →
🚆
Train tickets — how to buy

No ticket office at St Albans City station — just machines on the platform and the app. Buy via the Thameslink app, Trainline, or tap in with a contactless card. No Oyster gates, but inspectors check on board.

thameslinkrailway.com → nationalrail.co.uk →
💳
16-25 Railcard — buy this first

£30 a year, saves a third off most rail fares. Pays for itself in two or three London trips. Buy online or from the ticket machine at the station. Essential if you're using the train regularly.

railcard.co.uk →

The full picture

From Francis Avenue you can reach central London in under 35 minutes door to door, Brighton in under 2 hours, and Watford, Luton, Harpenden or Hemel Hempstead in under 45 minutes — all without a car. The Ancient Briton is one of the better-served residential stops in the city. Make the most of it.
